I N F L U E N C E R S


Summer is one of the busiest travel seasons of the year, which means going a vacation during this time takes a little extra planning. Before taking a trip, you need to think about what destinations aren't going to be overly crowded, too hot, or super expensive, since airfare costs often spike during summer months. With so many factors to consider when planning a summer vacation, we turned to travel influencers to see where they recommend going this June.

Ahead, you'll find seven June destination recommendations. These influencers suggest both domestic and international locations, so you can decide what works best for your travel goals and budgets. They've also provided tips for where to stay, which sites to see, and what to eat in order to kick off the summer travel season the right way.

Malaysia

"Malaysia is a great place to visit for many reasons. First of all, there's something for everyone. If you prefer a big city vibe, keep Kuala Lumpur in mind. From skyscrapers to mega-malls, KL has much to offer. Including street markets reminiscent of Thailand and bat cave adventures.

If you're a little bit country, head to the Cameron Highlands. Known for its tea, trekking, and temples. Soak up some sun and do a bit of island hopping (Tioman or Langkawi) or check out the UNESCO World Heritage site of Penang.

Penang is one of my personal favourite destinations. There's so much colour and culture in this historic city. Indian, Chinese, and Malay people make up a majority of the population. Which is evident in the food, architecture, and traditions. Be sure to try the nutmeg juice, it's a delicious and unique flavour that you must try in Penang."

Quebec City, Canada

"Although Canada is known for its cold winters, come springtime Canada is marvellous. June makes the perfect time to visit the beautiful Quebec City. Filled with charm, Old Quebec has the feel of cottage country. If you're looking for an escape from the busy city, you'll be in awe of QC.

Firstly, this is the home of poutine. You'll find countless restaurants that specialise in various forms of this delicious treat. Quebec is also known for its maple syrup and wine. A perfect day is walking from shop to shop tasting all these delicacies.

If you feel fancy, you can stay at the Fairmount Le Chateau Frontenac, which is an actual castle. If you are into history, you will love the stunning architecture. Take advantage of daily walking tours to learn about the history of Quebec City and brush up on your French. You don't need a car because you can walk everywhere inside the Walled City, or even take a horse and carriage ride. It is the ultimate relaxing getaway."

Sun Valley, Idaho

"Mountains beckon and adventure awaits in the stunning Sun Valley, Idaho. Forever a favourite with the Hollywood Elite, this mountain town has a peaceful presence where visitors can take a digital detox and enjoy the sound of silence. While it's known as 'America's First Destination Ski Resort,' Sun Valley truly shines in the month of June — and with 200 days of sunshine a year, I truly mean shines.

Visitors to Sun Valley should stay at the famed Sun Valley Lodge and dine at local favourite restaurants such as The Ram, Gretchen's, and Grumpy's. With its infinite adventures, visitors to Sun Valley can go hiking on the Proctor Mountain Loop, mountain biking on Curly's Loop, horseback riding at the Sun Valley Stables, and you can even go ice skating all year round at the Sun Valley Lodge. If you're feeling extra audacious, head to the top of Bald Mountain aka "Baldy," where you can paraglide from 9,000 feet of altitude with Fly Sun Valley! After a full day of adventures, seek out some of the healing hot springs in the Sun Valley area to soothe your muscles and your soul.

With direct flights from Los Angeles, San Francisco, Chicago, Seattle, Denver, Portland, and Salt Lake City, Sun Valley is easily accessible from many parts of the United States."

New York

"Before the dreaded humidity of the late summer starts to set in, visiting New York City in June proves to be the best month weather-wise. Along with the sunshine comes the city starting to celebrate the warmer temperatures, so getting outside and walking around will create the perfect trip.

Shakespeare in the Park starts around this time of year, so you can get your Bard on for free at Central Park. The Mermaid Parade on Coney Island shows off a colourful cast of characters that will bring you joy. At the end of the month, NYC Pride is one of the biggest celebrations of the LGBTQ+ community in the world, so you'll find people from all over the planet in town to party it up.

One of my favourite parts of the city is the Chinatown and Soho neighbourhoods, where you can find some of the best and most Instagrammable food shops, so don't miss out on pink restaurants, intricate latte art, cereal infused ice cream, and more photogenic treats in this area of Manhattan."

Florence, Italy

"Ah, Florence. I'd recommend visiting this stunning Tuscan heritage city in June. The summer weather jumps to 30 degrees celsius, and the entire city feels like an open-air museum, with world-famous art and Renaissance architecture on every corner. Spend your days wandering medieval cobbled alleys and museums, watching local artists paint watercolours on the Ponte Vecchio, window-shopping at jewellery boutiques, and inhaling the heady smell of Italian leather at the marketplace with a gelato in hand. By night, explore Florence's vibrant aperitivo bar culture and enjoy some of the finest Tuscan food in the region. The city was built for walking and discovering — perfect for a June weekend away."

Mexico City, Mexico

"Mexico City is one of our favourites spots to visit in June. As their hot months' end, June in Mexico City is ideal for the perfect weather to walk around the shops in Polanco or to visit their beautiful landmarks like the Chapultepec Castle or Pyramid of the Sun, which are filled with the Mexican history.

Mexico not only offers you a variety of activities to do, but Mexico City is top in our culinary spots to visit. Not only will you enjoy traditional Mexican dishes that are to die for, but Mexico City has been known to tackle all types of cuisines and master them."
