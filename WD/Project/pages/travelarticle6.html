S U M M E R


Day 1:
The German Motorbike Route covers the country’s most stunning motorbiking regions, offering a variety of routes past biker gatherings, automobile museums and biker-friendly stays. Begin at Zwickau. Close to Leipzig Airport, it is the birthplace of composer Robert Schumann and home to automotive brands like Horch (check out the museum) and Audi (it’s a three-hour drive to the Volkswagen factory in Wolfsburg).

Day 2:
Drive out to Chemnitz, where the Museum of Saxon Vehicles has nearly 200 exhibits showcasing the century-old regional heritage of car, motorbike and bicycle manufacturing.

Day 3:
In Dresden, admire the Baroque beauty of Zwinger Palace and visit the transport museum that showcases railway, aviation, shipping and road vehicles such as 1885’s Daimler Reitwagen, the first internal combustion motorcycle.

Day 4:
Hamburg calls with its bustling harbour, traditional market and century-old fish auction hall. Summer brings 50,000 bikers for the Hamburg Harley Days rally—three days of live music and parades.

Day 5: The road to Ibbenbüren skirts pretty villages and woods, with a gem of a bike museum whose displays include Germany’s ultimate 1970s superbike, the Münch Mammut 1200 TTS.

B I K I N G    I N    G E R M A N Y

Day 6:
Zip to Schottenring, a former motorsport race track built in 1925 in the volcanic Vogelsberg Mountains that can now be driven on as it is a public road.

Day 7:
Next, to Speyer, for its magnificent Imperial Cathedral and its Technology Museum with vintage bikes, a U9 submarine, and one of the largest exhibitions on manned space flights.

Day 8:
Head out to Stuttgart, which is home to The Mercedes-Benz Museum and The Porsche Museum. This is historic ground; it was in the outskirts of Stuttgart, after all, that the first ‘true’ motorbike was taken for a spin.

Day 9:
Ride on to Ingolstadt for tours at the Audi Forum and downtime in a Bavarian beer garden.

Day 10:
Hit the road and drive out to Munich’s BMW Museum and slot in a football match at Allianz Arena; if you want to celebrate full-throttle, time your visit to Oktoberfest.

Originally written by Raj Aditya Chaudhuri
Compiled from CN Traveller
